PERUR PATEESWARAR

HISTORY:
The Perur Pateeswarar Temple, located in Coimbatore, was built in the 2nd century AD by King Karikala Chola. It is a 2,000-year-old temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known as Pateeswarar. Legend states a divine cow named Kamadhenu's calf stepped on a lingam here, drawing blood, which led to the temple's construction. The temple is also known for its ancient architectural style, detailed sculptures, and spiritual significance, especially for performing rituals for the deceased.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Sweets and savories: Traditional South Indian sweets like Mysore Pak and halwa are available for purchase.
Variety rice: You can buy various pre-prepared rice dishes.
Prasadams: Temple-offered prasadam is distributed to visitors, particularly after the morning puja.
Local products: Other local products like handicrafts may be available for purchase from vendors around the temple.
TIME:
Morning: 5:00 AM to 1:00 PM
Evening: 4:00 PM to 9:00 PM
Note: Some sources mention the evening closing time as 8:30 PM or 8:00 PM.
PEAK HOURS:
The peak times at Perur Pateeswarar Temple are generally during the morning and evening daily darshan times, as well as during the special festivals, particularly the annual Brahmotsavam, and on auspicious days like Masi Magam. The temple is very popular, so these times can be crowded.
SPECIALITY
The Perur Pateeswarar Temple is special for its Dravidian architecture, which includes intricate carvings and the Kanaka Sabha hall with a gold-plated statue of Lord Nataraja. It is also notable for its mythological significance, such as the legend of the divine cow Kamadhenu, whose footprint is believed to be on the deity, and the unique Irava Panai and Pirava Puli trees. Additionally, the temple is historically significant, with ancient inscriptions and a connection to the Chola dynasty.

MARUTHAMALAI HILL

HISTORY:
The Maruthamalai hill temple in Coimbatore, dedicated to Lord Murugan, has origins in the ancient Sangam period and was referenced in texts like the Purananuru. The current temple structure dates back to the 12th century, with later additions by rulers like the Cholas, Pandyas, and Nayakas. The hill is named after the native Marudham (Arjuna) trees, which are abundant in the area and are known for their medicinal properties.
FAMOUS THINGS:
religious offerings like garlands and coconuts, and sacred prasadam such as puffed rice with jaggery (pori) and vadai
TIME:
Timings are from 5.30 a.m. to 1 p.m. and 2 p.m. to 8.30 p.m.
PEAK HOURS:
Weekends: Crowds are significantly higher on Saturdays and Sundays. Festival days: Major festivals like Thai Poosam (January-February), Panguni Uthiram (March-April), and Skanda Sashti (October-November) attract thousands of devotees. Auspicious days: Tuesdays and other government holidays can also see a marked increase in visitors.
SPECIALITY:
The Maruthamalai Murugan Temple is special due to its ancient origins, scenic hill location in the Western Ghats, and its association with Lord Murugan's legend. It is a 12th-century temple built on a hill named after the medicinal Marudham (Arjuna) trees, with a history stretching back to the Sangam period. The temple is also known for its beautiful architecture, the inclusion of a shrine to Pambatti Siddhar, and is considered a significant spiritual site for its perceived ability to bless devotees with protection, wisdom, and health.

GASS FOREST MUSEUM

HISTORY
The Gass Forest Museum in Coimbatore was established in 1902 by Horace Archibald Gass, the then Conservator of Forests, and was originally opened on April 15, 1902. It was renamed the Gass Forest Museum in recognition of Gass's efforts, and the present building was inaugurated on October 14, 1915, by the Governor of Madras, Lord Pentland. The museum has served as a repository for forestry-related exhibits and has been a central location for forest research and education in India for over a century.
FAMOUS THINGS:
You can't purchase items directly from the Gass Forest Museum in Coimbatore, but you can buy souvenir postcards depicting the museum's famous exhibits, and special postal covers were released in 2018 featuring the museum and its artifacts.
TIME:
Morning: 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM
Afternoon: 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM
Closed: Saturdays, Sundays, and Central Government Holidays
PEAK HOURS:
Early weekday mornings, particularly around 9 AM, are the busiest. Weekend mornings are also very popular with families and school groups.
SPECIALITY:
The Gass Forest Museum in Coimbatore is unique for its large collection of stuffed animals, timber samples, and artifacts, all housed in a historic 100-year-old Gothic-style building. Key unique features include a large 3D diorama of a forest with sculpted animals, a diverse collection of insect specimens, and significant displays of various timber species, making it a rich and educational experience. It's one of the oldest and largest forest museums in the country and is located on the grounds of the Forest College Campus.

ARULMIGU EACHANARI VINAYAGAR TEMPLE

HISTORY:
The Arulmigu Eachanari Vinayagar Temple is believed to have originated around the 16th century, with its giant idol of Lord Ganesha being placed here after a cart axle broke during transport from Madurai. The temple was later consecrated, with its first major consecration taking place in 1977 and subsequent ones in 1990. It is a significant religious site in Coimbatore, known for its large idol and the daily "Ganapathi Homam".
FAMOUS THINGS :
Prasadam: This is the traditional holy food offering made to Lord Ganesha.
Coconuts: Devotees frequently buy coconuts to break as part of the "drishti" ritual, a ceremony to ward off the evil eye.
Pooja items: Shops at the temple complex sell various items needed for worship.
TIME:
Opening and Closing: 5:00 AM to 10:00 PM daily.
Pooja Timings: While the temple is open for darshan continuously, some sources mention poojas are scheduled from 5:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 6:00 PM to 10:00 PM
PEAK HOURS:
Festivals: The 10-day Ganesh Chaturthi is the most crowded time. Other major festivals like Aadi Perukku, Saraswathy Pooja, Thai Pongal, Deepawali, and Tamil New Year also attract large numbers of visitors.
Peak hours: Mornings and evenings are generally crowded due to their spiritual significance.
SPECIALITY:
The Eachanari Vinayagar Temple's specialty is its massive 6-foot-high idol of Lord Vinayagar, which is said to be self-installed. The temple is also known for its Dravidian-style architecture, a peaceful atmosphere, and special pujas like the daily Ganapathy Homam. The idol's origin story, involving a cart breaking down near the site, also contributes to its uniqueness.

VICTORIA HALL

HISTORY: Coimbatore's Victoria Hall, now the Town Hall, was built in 1892 and named after Queen Victoria. It served as a venue for public meetings, civic receptions, and banquets, famously hosting figures like Mahatma Gandhi and Rajaji. The building faced demolition in the 1990s but was saved by the efforts of the Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage (INTACH) and has since been restored and is used for Corporation council meetings.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Textiles: Coimbatore is famous for its cotton industry, so look for clothing made from local cotton.
Traditional Items: Explore the area for traditional South Indian products, handmade gifts, and accessories.
Handmade Gifts: Consider shops like Sri Lakshmi Agencies for unique handmade gift boxes.
Kanjivaram Silk Sarees: While you may not find them at a street vendor, the region's textile heritage makes it a great place to shop for traditional and popular silk sarees with intricate designs.
TIMING:
10 am-5 pm
PEAK HOURS:
business hours (mornings to evenings, especially evenings for shopping) and festivals, though the Hall itself is for official functions, so its internal busy times depend on government schedules, not public tourism.
SPECIALITY:
The Victoria Town Hall in Coimbatore is known for its neoclassical and historical architecture, serving as a landmark in the city's "Townhall" area. It was originally named after Queen Victoria and has hosted significant public events, civic receptions, and gatherings, including speeches by historical figures like Mahatma Gandhi and Rajaji. Its speciality lies in its historical significance as a center for civic life and its role in the city's heritage conservation efforts, as it was saved from demolition in the 1990s.
