VEERA RAGHAVA SWAMY TEMPLE

HISTORY:
The Veera Raghava Swamy Temple in Tiruvallur, a significant 108 Divya Desam, dates back to the Pallava era (8th century), with later contributions from Cholas and Vijayanagara kings, housing a reclining Vishnu known as Vaidya Veeraraghavan, believed to be a divine healer who appeared to sage Salihotra after his penance, offering boons to devotees, especially regarding health, with its unique architecture and sacred tank (Hruthapadhani) adding to its historical and spiritual importance.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Prasadam, small idols, devotional books, and sacred threads (nada).
TIME:
Morning: 5:00 AM to 1:00 PM (with ritual breaks).
Afternoon/Evening: 4:00 PM/4:30 PM to 8:30 PM/9:00 PM.
PEAK HOUR:
New Moon Days (Amavasya), Saturdays, and during major festivals like the Chittirai Brahmotsavam.
SPECIALITY:
The Veeraraghava Swamy Temple in Thiruvallur is special for its deity, Vaidya Veeraraghavan, believed to cure all diseases, especially when worshippers offer metal cutouts of afflicted body parts and bathe in the sacred Hritayathabhanasini tank, considered holier than the Ganga, granting relief from ailments, sins, and hardships, and promising boons like marriage and progeny, all within a significant Dravidian temple structure praised by Azhvar saints.

TRIPURANTAKESWARAR TEMPLE

HISTORY:
The Tripurantakeswara Temple (also known as Thirupuranthakeswarar Temple) in Tiruvallur, Tamil Nadu, is an ancient Shiva temple where Lord Shiva is worshipped as the destroyer of the three cities (Tripuras), earning him the name 'Tripuranthakeswara'; it's a Paadal Petra Sthalam, praised by Saint Tirugnanasambandar, featuring a unique Gajabrushta Vimana and separate shrines for Goddess Parvati and Nataraja, with legends linking it to ancient battles and divine interventions, making it a significant heritage site with distinctive architectural and religious features.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Decorative diyas, pooja sets, religious books (Gita, Ramayana), brass items, framed deity photos, and local artisan crafts.
TIME:
Morning: 6:00 AM to 12:00 PM (Noon)
Evening: 5:00 PM to 7:00 PM (or sometimes until 8:30 PM)
PEAK HOUR:
weekends, and major Hindu pilgrimage seasons.
SPECIALITY:
The Tripurantakeswara Temple in Tiruvallur (Cooum) is special as a Paadal Petra Sthalam, praised in Thevaram hymns, known for its unique Shiva Lingam that turns red before untoward events, and features a Nataraja performing the "Kathal Thandavam" (dance of love). It's considered an Agni (fire) sthalam, linked to the legend of Lord Shiva destroying the Tripura demons, and is surrounded by fertile lands, with the Cooum River nearby.

PULICAT LAKE

HISTORY:
Pulicat, or Pazhaverkadu, boasts a rich history as an ancient port on India's Coromandel Coast, flourishing from Chola times with Arab traders and later becoming the Dutch East India Company's key hub, their capital for Dutch Coromandel, featuring Fort Geldria. Its significance as a center for textiles and spices attracted Europeans, with Portuguese establishing early Christian presence before the Dutch dominance in the 17th century, eventually losing prominence to British-driven Madras but leaving behind unique multi-religious heritage sites like Dutch monuments, Portuguese cemeteries, and ancient temples.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Palm Leaf Products.
Seafood.
TIME:
Hour: 6 AM to 6 PM.
PEAK HOUR:
Winter months, from October to March.
SPECIALITY:
Pulicat's speciality lies in being India's second-largest brackish water lagoon, a vital bird sanctuary famous for migratory birds like flamingos, and a historic trade port with Dutch colonial remnants, offering rich eco-tourism with boating, seafood, and unique cultural heritage.

GOUDIE MEMORIAL CHURCH

HISTORY:
The Goudie Memorial Church (often associated with Ikkadu/Tiruvallur area in India) honors William Goudie, a dedicated Methodist missionary who arrived in 1883, known for his compassion for the poor and untouchables, particularly in rural Tamil Nadu, with the church serving as a testament to his profound missionary impact and legacy in spreading Christianity and establishing local congregations, especially among marginalized communities.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Candles.
Hymnals/Bibles.
Religious items.
TIME:
6:00 am-8:00 pm
PEAK HOUR:
Major Festivals.
Sunday Services.
SPECIALITY:
The CSI Goudie Memorial Church in Ikkadu, Tiruvallur, is known for its historical significance as a Christian worship center, particularly within the CSI (Church of South India) denomination, serving its local community with regular services, though specific unique architectural or historical features aren't detailed in the initial snippets, its main specialty lies in its role as a local spiritual hub for the community in Periya Edapalayam, Tiruvallur.

TIRUTTANI ARULMIGU SUBRAMANIYA SWAMI TEMPLE

HISTORY:
The Tiruttani Arulmigu Subramaniya Swami Temple, located in the Tiruvallur district, is a significant ancient hill temple dedicated to Lord Muruga, one of the Arupadaiveedu (six abodes). Its history is linked to Lord Muruga cooling off here after defeating the demon Surapadman, and it's known for its 365 steps representing the days of the year, offering spiritual solace and blessings, with beliefs that worshipping for five days brings immense fortune.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Flowers, 'Vel' (spears), and milk for abhishekam, alongside holy ash (Vibuthi) .
TIME:
opens around 5:30 AM and closes around 8:30 PM (weekdays) or 9:00 PM (weekends).
PEAK HOUR:
Thaipusam (Jan/Feb), Aadi Krittikai (July/Aug), and special occasions like the New Year (Dec 31st).
SPECIALITY:
Tiruttani Temple, one of the six sacred abodes of Lord Murugan (Arupadaiveedu), is special for being a place of peace (Shantipuri) where Murugan married Valli, features 365 steps symbolizing the year, and houses the deity in a calm, weaponless form, signifying the quelling of his anger after defeating demons, offering devotees immense peace and victory.
