ST GEORGE FORT

HISTORY:
Fort St. George in Chennai, built in 1639-1640, is the first English fort in India and the birthplace of modern Chennai. Initially a trading post for the East India Company, it grew into a symbol of British power, enduring attacks and becoming the administrative and military center of the Madras Presidency. Today, it houses the Tamil Nadu government's Legislative Assembly and Secretariat, as well as a museum and the St. Mary's Church.
FAMOUS THINGS:
textiles (such as Godown Street's textiles), electrical goods, pens and stationery, dry fruits, steel hardware, and plastic products.
TIME:
Daily Hours: 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM
Closed: Fridays
PEAK HOURS:
Morning and late afternoon are typically busier times.
SPECIALITY: Fort St. George's speciality lies in its historical significance as the first English fortress in India, its role as the administrative headquarters of the Tamil Nadu Government, and its numerous attractions.

KAPALEESHWARAR TEMPLE

HISTORY:
The Kapaleeshwarar Temple in Mylapore, Chennai, has a history dating back to the 7th century, though its current structure was rebuilt in the 16th century after being destroyed by the Portuguese. The original temple was possibly built by the Pallava dynasty. Legend states the temple's name comes from a story involving Lord Shiva and the "kapalam" (skull) of Lord Brahma. The area, known as Mylapore, is also linked to a legend of Goddess Parvati performing penance as a peahen.
FAMOUS THINGS:
puja items, devotional books, brassware, silk sarees, and traditional handicrafts like clay dolls and paintings.
TIME:
Morning: 5:00 AM to 12:00 PM (Some sources say 5:30 AM)
Evening: 4:00 PM to 9:00 PM (Some sources say 9:30 PM)
PEAK HOURS:
The peak hours at Kapaleeshwarar Temple are typically during the morning and evening prayer times and especially during major festivals like Maha Shivaratri or Panguni Uthiram, when crowds are heaviest.
SPECIALITY:
The Kapaleeshwarar temple's specialities include its magnificent Dravidian architecture featuring a towering, sculpture-covered gopuram, and its historical connection to Lord Shiva and Goddess Karpagambal, with a legend of Parvati performing penance there. It is also renowned for its numerous shrines within the complex, the Punnai tree, and being a major cultural and spiritual center in Chennai's historic Mylapore neighborhood.

VIVEKANANDA HOUSE

HISTORY:
Vivekananda House, originally known as Castle Kernan or Ice House, was built in 1842 and became historically significant after Swami Vivekananda stayed there in 1897. He delivered several lectures during his nine-day stay, which inspired the founding of the Sri Ramakrishna Math in Chennai at the same location from 1897 to 1906. The building was renamed Vivekanandar Illam in 1963 and now serves as the Experience Vivekananda Museum.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Books on the life and teachings of Swami Vivekananda and Indian philosophy/religion. Souvenirs such as mugs, keychains, and T-shirts, often featuring Swami Vivekananda's quotes.
TIME:
Opening Hours: 10 AM -12:30 PM and 3 PM - 7:15 PM
Closed: Mondays
Sundays and special days: Closes at 7:30 PM
PEAK HOURS:
Morning: Around 10:00 AM to 12:30 PM
Afternoon: Around 3:00 PM to 6:00 PM
SPECIALITY:
Vivekananda House's speciality is its historical connection to Swami Vivekananda, who stayed there in 1897 after his return from the West, and its current function as the Experience Vivekananda Museum. The building, originally known as the Ice House, served as the first center for the Ramakrishna Math in South India and now features a museum with a modern, immersive experience of his life and message.

GOVERNMENT MUSEUM

HISTORY:
The Government Museum in Chennai, formerly the Madras Museum, was established in 1851, making it the second oldest museum in India. Initially located in a building on College Road, it was moved to its current site in Egmore in 1854. The museum's history began with a proposal by the Madras Literary Society in 1846 to create a museum in the area.
FAMOUS THINGS:

The largest number of Roman antiquities outside of Europe, a rich collection of South Indian bronzes (especially Chola bronzes), and prehistoric artifacts from South India.
TIME:
Opening Time: 9:30 AM
Closing Time: 5:00 PM
Closed: Fridays and national holidays
PEAK HOURS:
11:00 AM to 2:00 PM
SPECIALITY:
The speciality of the Government Museum, Chennai (also known as Madras Museum) is its vast and diverse collection, especially its South Indian bronze sculptures and Roman antiquities, making it one of the largest museums in South Asia. It is also the second oldest museum in India and a major cultural hub, featuring the Connemara Public Library and a museum theater, all spread across six buildings.

SAN THOME CATHEDRAL BASILICA

HISTORY:
The San Thome Cathedral Basilica is a historic Roman Catholic minor basilica in Chennai, built in the 16th century by Portuguese explorers over the tomb of Saint Thomas, one of Jesus's twelve apostles. The church has been rebuilt and renovated multiple times, with the current Neo-Gothic structure completed in 1896 by the British. It is one of only three churches in the world built over the tomb of an apostle.
FAMOUS THINGS:
fridge magnets featuring the cathedral's image and other religious items.
TIME:
Weekdays-5.45AM-5.45PM
SUNDAYS-6.00AM-6.00PM
PEAK HOURS:
The San Thome Cathedral Basilica does not have designated "peak hours," but it is busiest during mass times, especially on Sundays at 6 AM, 7:15 AM, 8:30 AM, 10 AM, 11:30 AM, and 6 PM. Weekday masses are also crowded, particularly the morning and evening services held at 6:15 AM, 5:45 PM, and 6:15 PM.
SPECIALITY:
The specialty of the San Thome Cathedral Basilica is that it is one of only three churches in the world built over the tomb of an apostle of Jesus. It is a Neo-Gothic architectural masterpiece, built in the 16th century over the tomb of Saint Thomas, and features elaborate interiors with stained-glass windows and a tall spire. It is an important historical and pilgrimage site for Christians.
