SRI CHORNAKALEESWARAR TEMPLE

HISTORY:
Kalaiyar Temple is an ancient Shiva temple in Sivaganga district; it is also known as Chornakaleeswarar Temple, and is the place where Sundarar and Sambandar sang the Thevaram; the name "Kalaiyar Temple" came about because Shiva appeared in the form of a bull here and gave a vision to Sundarar; due to the renovation of the Marudhu Pandyas, it stands out with its twin towers, similar to the Madurai Meenakshi Amman Temple Gopuram.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Handicrafts: Items reflecting local artistry, often sold in surrounding Sivaganga district areas.
Textiles: Beautiful silks and cottons, typical of Tamil Nadu.
Religious Items: Small idols, pooja items, or souvenirs related to the presiding deities, Shiva (Kaaleeshwarar) and Parvati (Swarnambikai).
Local Snacks & Spices: Traditional Chettinad delicacies and spice blends.
TIME:
Morning: 6:00 AM - 12:00 PM
Evening: 4:00 PM - 9:00 PM
PEAK HOUR:
More people visit Kalaiyar Kovil during major Tamil festivals like Thai Poosam (Jan-Feb), Panguni Uththiram (Mar-Apr), Aadi Kirthigai (Jul-Aug), and the Karthigai Skandha Sashti (Oct-Nov), attracting large crowds for special pujas and temple car festivals.
SPECIALITY:
Kalaiyar Kovil Chornakaleeswarar Kovil is an ancient and special Shiva temple in Sivaganga. It is believed that one can attain salvation whether born or dead here; it is the 200th Shiva temple to have received the Thevaram hymn, the place where Kali was worshipped to get rid of her sins, the history of renovation by Marudhu Pandyas, and it has three halls (three halls) are its main features.

KUNDRAKUDI TEMPLE

HISTORY:
Kundrakudi Murugan Temple, or Shanmughanathar Temple, in Sivaganga, Tamil Nadu, is an ancient rock-cut shrine complex with origins dating back to the 8th-century Pandya period, famous for its unique rock-cut caves, early sculpted Dwarapalakas (door guardians), and its association with Lord Murugan blessing devotees with both consorts (Valli & Deivanai) on a single hill. The temple complex sits on a small hill, requiring a climb of about 150 steps, with shrines to Ganesha and Palani Murugan at the base, offering a rich blend of history and devotional significance.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Pooja items, offer salt & pepper for skin cures, and seek blessings through rituals like Kaavadi & Paal Kudam, fulfilling vows for health, marriage, and wishes.
TIME:
Morning: 6:00 AM - 11:00 AM
Evening: 4:00 PM - 8:00 PM
PEAK HOURS:
Panguni Uthiram: A grand 10-day festival in March-April.
Thaipoosam: Another major 10-day festival in January-February.
Other Festivals: Events like Skanda Sashti, Aadi Tirupadi Puja, and Aavani Moolam also draw significant crowds.
SPECIALITY:
Kundrakudi Murugan Temple's speciality lies in its unique hill setting resembling a peacock, housing Lord Murugan as Shanmughanathar (six faces), with rock-cut shrines, ancient paintings, and powerful legends of healing skin ailments, infertility, and granting marital bliss, attracting devotees for its spiritual significance and historical depth, praised by saints like Arunagirinathar.

SIVAGANGAI PALACE

HISTORY:
Sivaganga Palace, built around 1730 by Sasivarna Thevar, was the historic seat of Marava kings, famously home to Rani Velu Nachiyar, and a center for anti-British resistance with leaders like Veerapandiya Kattabomman. Though the original structure is gone, the present Gowri Vilasam, built in the early 19th century, showcases a unique blend of Nayaka and Rajputana styles, housing the intact Sri Raja Rajeshwari Temple and remnants of royal courts and coronations on black marble, symbolizing a proud, resilient heritage.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Handicrafts: Local artisans sell traditional crafts that capture the region's cultural essence.
Regional Items: You can find items reflecting the history of the Marava kings and Tamil heritage.
TIME:

Closed: May be closed on Sundays.
PEAK HOURS:
Summer (Apr-Jun): While hot (up to 42Â°C), it's also a period for summer vacations, bringing more local visitors, coinciding with cultural festivals, and offering chances to explore landscapes and wildlife.
SPECIALITY:
Sivagangai Palace, specifically the Gowri Vilasam, is special for its unique blend of Tirumala Nayak and Rajputana architecture, housing the functional Sri Raja Rajeshwari Temple, a historic Durbar Hall, and unique features like the Nadai Kinaru (miniature pool), serving as a significant symbol of Rani Velu Nachiyar's resistance against the British and the Marava rulers' heritage.

MARUDUPANDIYAN FORT

HISTORY:
The Sivaganga Fort, stronghold of the Maruthu Pandiyar brothers, was central to their fierce resistance against the British East India Company in the late 18th/early 19th centuries, serving as a base for secret anti-British planning with Veerapandiya Kattabomman and a site for battles, ultimately witnessing their execution in 1801 at Tirupputhur Fort, marking a significant chapter in South Indian rebellion.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Chettinad Mixture: A spicy, crunchy mix of puffed rice, sev, peanuts, and curry leaves, a staple in the region.
Murukku: Crispy, spiral-shaped rice flour snacks, often made fresh.
Adhirasam: A traditional deep-fried sweet made from rice flour and jaggery.
TIME:
10.00 A.M - 6.00 P.M
PEAK HOUR:
More people visit Sivaganga's significant sites, including places related to the Maruthu Pandiyars, during the Maruthu Pandiyar Day (October 24th/27th) for commemorative events at Kalayarkoil (where they are buried) and during major Tamil festivals like Thai Poosam or Panguni Uththiram.
SPECIALITY:
The speciality of Sivaganga's Maruthu Pandiyar Fort lies in its deep connection to the legendary Marudhu brothers, Periya Marudhu and Chinna Marudhu, who were fierce freedom fighters leading early rebellions against the British East India Company, making it a symbol of South Indian resistance, strategic guerrilla warfare (using the unique Valari weapon), and administration for the welfare of Sivaganga Seemai (region) before their martyrdom in 1801.

SRI SOWMYANARAYANA PERUMAL TEMPLE

HISTORY:
Thirukoshtiyur Sowmya Narayana Perumal Temple is an ancient, significant Vishnu temple, one of the 108 Divya Desams, known for its unique tiered Vimana and history involving the Deities meeting to combat Hiranyakashipu, Ramanuja's revelation of the Ashtakshara mantra, and inscriptions dating to the 9th century. The temple features Lord Vishnu as Sowmya Narayana Perumal, worshipped in different postures across its levels, and is a key pilgrimage site for Vaishnavites, glorifying the names of several Alvars.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Earthen Lamps (Diyas)
Prasadam
Puja Samagri (Worship Items)
TIME:
Morning: 7:00 AM - 12:00 PM
Evening: 4:00 PM - 7:30 PM (or 8:00 PM)
PEAK HOUR:
More people visit Thirukoshtiyur Sowmya Narayana Perumal Temple during major festivals like the Brahmotsavam in March/April, the Float Festival (Masi Makam) in February-March, Navratri (Purattasi) in September-October, and Vaikunta Ekadasi in December-January.
SPECIALITY:
Thirukoshtiyur Sowmya Narayana Perumal Temple's main specialties are its unique three-tiered shrine for Lord Vishnu (representing Earth, Vaikuntha, and Tiruparkadal), the historic moment Ramanuja preached the Ashtakshara Mantra to all castes from the temple walls, and its status as one of the 108 Divya Desams, featuring a rare Ashtanga Vimana, shrines for Shiva and Narasimha, and legends related to the Narasimha Avatar.
