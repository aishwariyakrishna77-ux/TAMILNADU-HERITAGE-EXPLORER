KASI VISWANATHAR TEMPLE

HISTORY:
The Kasi Viswanathar Temple in Tenkasi, known as the "South Kasi," was built in the 13th-14th century by Pandya King Parakrama Pandian, who built it after Lord Shiva appeared in his dream, guiding him to build a shrine here to avoid the long pilgrimage to Kasi. The temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva (Kasi Viswanathar) and Goddess Ulagamman, features impressive Dravidian architecture, including a massive gopuram, and is significant as a holy site worshipped by sages like Agasthya and Narada.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Religious Items: Vibhuti (sacred ash), kumkum, holy water (theertham), and rudraksha beads.
Pooja Items: Small brass lamps, bells, or containers for offerings.
Textiles: Cotton or silk scarves, handkerchiefs, often with religious motifs.
TIME:
Morning: 6:00 AM – 12:00 PM
Evening: 4:00 PM – 8:30 PM (or 8:00 PM)
PEAK HOURS:
Weekends (Saturdays & Sundays): Expect more devotees.
Festival Days: During major festivals like Brahmostavam (May-June) and Sivarathri, expect large crowds.
New Moon Days (Amavasai): The temple often stays open all day, attracting more visitors.
During Daily Rituals: Around 8:30 AM, 11:30 AM, and 5:30 PM when poojas are performed.
SPECIALITY:
The Kasi Viswanathar Temple in Tenkasi is special for its towering 180-foot gopuram, its status as the "Kasi of the South," musical stone pillars, and unique sculptures, including the Virabhadra figures and a west-facing Durga, blending Dravidian architecture with deep spiritual significance as a connection to the sacred city of Kashi.

THIRUMALAI KUMARASWAMY.

HISTORY:
The Thirumalai Kumaraswamy Temple, a historic hilltop shrine for Lord Murugan near Tenkasi, dates back centuries, with its idol discovered around 600 years ago after appearing in a priest's dream, leading to its installation in the current structure, possibly built by Thiruvarutchelvar Sivakami Ammaiyar, and featuring rock-cut shrines, inscriptions from the Pandyan era, and Arunagirinathar's praise in Thiruppugal, making it a significant site for history and devotion.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Sweets (Mithai): Peda (a milk-based sweet), Jaggery Laddu, and regular Laddu are popular choices.
Temple Prasadam: Enjoying the traditional food offered at the temple is a key experience, often including payasam (a sweet pudding).
Snacks & Tea: You'll find small stalls selling tea, coffee, and light snacks for refreshments.
TIME:
Morning: 6:00 AM - 1:30 PM
Evening: 4:00 PM/4:30 PM - 8:00 PM/8:30 PM (check locally for exact times)
PEAK HOURS:
Weekends: Saturdays and Sundays.
Festivals: Skanda Sashti (Oct/Nov), Thai Poosam (Jan/Feb), Vaikasi Visakam (May/June), and Step Festival (Chithirai).
Peak Tourist Season: Around Courtallam, as it's nearby.
SPECIALITY:
Thirumalai Kumaraswamy Temple's specialties include its hilltop location amidst the Western Ghats, its 624 steps (though now accessible by road), being the Nakshatra temple for the Vishakha star, its unique spring (Saptha Kannika Teertham) with a divine lotus, and hymns by Arunagirinathar. The presiding deity, Lord Murugan, is depicted with four hands, and the temple is surrounded by coconut groves, offering serene views and a divine atmosphere.

SANKARANKOVIL TEMPLE.

HISTORY:
Sankarankovil Temple, built by Pandya King Ukkira Pandiyan in the 10th century CE, is famous for its unique deity, Sankaranarayana, a combined form of Shiva and Vishnu, symbolizing unity between Shaivism and Vaishnavism. Legend says Shiva appeared in this form to resolve a dispute between devotees, while another legend connects it to the serpent king Naga and the discovery of a self-manifested Shiva Lingam (Sankaralingam) from an anthill, whose earth is considered sacred and healing. The temple is also known for its grand Aadi Thabasu festival and Dravidian architecture, featuring a large Rajagopuram and a sanctum where sunlight falls on the deity during equinoxes, according to Trawell.in, Wikipedia, and Vedadhara.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Puja Items: Incense sticks, camphor, ghee, cotton wicks, brass/copper diyas, and small idols of deities.
Handicrafts & Souvenirs: Seek out local shops for wood carvings, small metal statues, and traditional Tamil Nadu crafts.
TIME:
Morning: 6:00 AM - 12:30 PM
Evening: 4:00 PM - 8:30 PM (or 9:00 PM)
PEAK HOURS:
The temple is usually busiest during the evening darshan hours, from approximately 4:00 PM to 9:00 PM on most days. Weekends (especially Sunday) and Fridays tend to be more consistently busy throughout the day compared to other weekdays.
The temple sees a significant increase in visitors during major festivals, particularly the Adi Tapasu festival (usually in July/August) and the last few months of the year (October-December).
SPECIALITY:
Sankarankovil Temple's main speciality is its unique idol of Sankaranarayana, a combined form of Lord Shiva (right side) and Lord Vishnu (left side), symbolizing their unity, along with shrines for Gomathi Amman (Parvati) and Sankaralinga Swamy (Shiva). The temple is famous for its holy ant-hill sand (Puttumann), believed to cure diseases, and its significant Adi Tapasu festival, where Gomathi Amman performs penance. It's also noted for its Dravidian architecture and historical links to the Pandya dynasty.

THIRUKUTALANATHA SWAMY TEMPLE.

HISTORY:
The Thirukutralanatha Swamy Temple, located in Courtallam near Tenkasi, is an ancient shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva with a history spanning approximately 2,000 years. It is highly significant as one of the 275 Paadal Petra Sthalams (holy abodes) of Lord Shiva and one of the five Pancha Sabhas (dance halls) where Lord Nataraja is said to perform his cosmic dance.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Local Snacks & Sweets: Traditional South Indian sweets and savories sold by vendors.
Herbal Products: Given Courtallam's medicinal waterfalls, some shops might offer herbal remedies or products, though this is less common than spiritual items.
Bangles & Costume Jewelry: Colorful bangles and simple jewelry are popular with visitors.
TIME:
Morning: 6:00 AM – 12:00 PM (Noon)
Evening: 4:30 PM – 8:00 PM (approx.)
PEAK HOURS:
Weekends: Saturdays and Sundays see more visitors.
Festival Days: Major festivals, especially the Aadi New Moon, draw larger crowds.
SPECIALITY:
The Thirukutrala Natha Swamy Temple in Tenkasi is special for its conch (Sangu) shape, legend of Sage Agastya converting a Vishnu temple to a Shiva shrine, being a Pancha Sabha Kshetram (Chitra Sabha), unique Chithra Sabha carvings, Adi Parasakthi Shakti Peetha, and its proximity to the Courtallam Waterfalls. Key features include the main deities Kutralanathar (Shiva) and Kuzhalvoi Mozhiammai (Parvati), the Kurumpala (Jackfruit) sacred tree, and its rich stone inscriptions detailing its ancient history.

AINTHARUVI.

HISTORY:
Aintharuvi (Five Falls) at Courtallam is a popular waterfall where water splits into five streams, resembling the five heads of the serpent deity Adisesha, and is known for its medicinal properties from waters flowing through herbs in the Western Ghats. It's a significant spot in the larger Courtallam Falls complex, distinct from the main falls (Peraruvi), offering different bathing experiences and attracting many tourists for its natural beauty and supposed healing waters, especially during the monsoon season.
FAMOUS THINGS:
Forest Fruits: Stalls near the falls often sell seasonal fruits like lychees, eggfruit, and mangosteen.
Medicinal & Herbal Products: Since Courtallam is known as the "Spa of South India," shops sell oils and herbal powders infused with forest herbs for health benefits.
Handicrafts: Wooden carvings, stone sculptures, and brassware are available.
TIME:
Open 24 hours
PEAK HOURS:
Monsoon Season: Mid-June to September, when water flow is highest.
Weekends & Holidays: Always busier than weekdays.
Saral Vila (Festival): Late July to early August.
SPECIALITY:
Five Falls (Aintharuvi) in Courtallam is special for its water splitting into five streams, resembling a five-headed cobra, offering distinct bathing areas for men (three falls) and women (two falls) with perceived medicinal benefits from Podhigai Hills herbs, alongside nearby temples and an Eco Park, making it a popular, scenic, and religiously significant spot in the "Spa of South India".
